Blackberry bramble jelly

About the bake

This blackberry bramble jelly is perfect to make in the early Autumn when the blackberries are in season.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

1.4kg Blackberries

1.5kg White granulated sugar

110g Silver Spoon White Caster Sugar

120ml Water

250ml Apple pectin

1 Lemon

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Place the blackberries, water and lemon juice in a preserving pan, bring to the boil. Add the apple pectin then simmer and a low heat for 20 minutes.

Step 2:
Tip the mixture into a jelly bag and leave to drip overnight

Step 3:
Add the water or enough water to make two pints of liquid mixture. Place back in the preserving pan with the sugar and heat over a low heat until all the sugar has dissolved, approximately 10-15 minutes.

Step 4:
Skim any scum from the top of jelly and fill sterilised jam jars. Cover, seal and label.

Total time: 35mins

Chocolate Mousse

About the bake

Why should you make this Chocolate Mousse? It takes 15 minutes to make, uses only two ingredients (eggs and chocolate) and makes a classy dessert for four people. The key to this Chocolate Mousse recipe is to make sure you get the home made Bain Marie (bowl over warming water in a saucepan) right. To high a heat and you will risk burning your chocolate. If you are not comfortable doing it that way, melt the chocolate in the microwave for around 30 seconds. We've found that letting your chocolate cool down until it is barely warm is the best way to avoid the warming the egg whites. The second secret to making a great Chocolate Mousse is to make sure you whip the egg whites until they are foamy and beginning to hold their shape. This ensures your Chocolate Mousse will be light and fluffy when you add in the chocolate. This recipe does use raw egg whites so do check that your guests are all ok eating this. When setting the Chocolate Mousse, we found that anywhere between 8 and 24 hours is enough time to allow them to set properly. Make sure you leave enough time in your schedule for setting.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

400g Dark chocolate (milk or white)

4 Egg white(s)

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Ceramic cups

Heatproof bowl

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Melt the chocolate in a heatproof bowl over boiling water (making sure that the water isn't touching the bowl)

Step 2:
Once the chocolate has melted, whisk the egg whites until firm.

Step 3:
Pour the egg white mixture into the chocolate, and mix well

Step 4:
Pour the mixture into 4 ceramic cups (or small glasses) and place in the fridge to set.

Total Time: 32mins
Prep Time: 20mins
Bake Time: 12mins
Serving: 6

Melt in the Middle Salted Caramel Pudding

About the bake

We’ve got the perfect indulgent recipe for a melt in the middle pudding. A soft chocolate sponge filled with indulgent warm, gooey salted caramel made with the finest Billington’s unrefined sugar. This bake has the real mmmmm factor when you take a bite. You can freeze your unbaked puds and simply bake straight from the freezer which is perfect if you’re after a quick-mid week dessert for the family.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

150g    SUnsalted butter

150g    Dark chocolate

2tsp     Nielsen-Massey Vanilla Extract

3  Large free range egg(s)

3   Free range large egg yolk(s)

150g   Caster Sugar

Utensils

6x 175ml/6oz dariole (pudding) moulds

Bowl

Whisk

Small saucepan

Method

Step 1:
Begin by making the caramel. In a saucepan place the butter, sugar, condensed milk, Dulce De Leche and sea salt. Heat all of the ingredients until the butter has melted.

Step 2:
Pour the caramel into a bowl and place in the fridge to chill and thicken for a few hours.

Step 3:
To make the chocolate sponges; preheat your oven to 200ºC/180ºC fan/Gas 6. Grease 6 175ml/6oz dariole moulds generously with butter

Step 4:
Melt the butter and chocolate in a large bowl over a pan of simmering water. Stir in the vanilla and set aside to cool

Step 5:
Whisk the eggs, egg yolk and sugar until pale and very thick

Step 6:
Gently fold the flour into the mixture.
