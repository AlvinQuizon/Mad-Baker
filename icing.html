Icing & Buttercream

Fluffy basic buttercream or frosting really is the icing on any cake. Simple to whip up, satisfying to gobble up. Master a buttercream recipe and experiment with flavours, or add cocoa for the best chocolate buttercream.
Prep Time: 5mins
Serving: 6

Glace Icing

About the bake

Glace Icing is smooth, runny and glossy and is ideal for sponges, fairy cakes, biscuits, cookies and finger buns. It sets hard and so it also makes an ideal edible glue (for those fiddly Gingerbread Houses).

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

125g Silver Spoon Icing Sugar

15ml Warm water

Utensils

Bowl

Method

Step 1:
Sift the icing sugar into a bowl and gradually add the warm water until the icing becomes thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. If necessary, add more water (a drop at a time) or icing sugar to adjust the consistency.

Prep Time: 20mins
Serving: 12

Meringue topped mince pie

About the bake

Tired of the same traditional mince pies - add a special twist to yours this Christmas by topping them with a light, fluffy meringue top.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

600ml    Double cream

75g Dark chocolate (chopped)

30g     Cocoa powder

4  Egg yolk(s)

1pc   Chocolate extract

115g  Caster Sugar

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Bowl

Mixer

Method

Step 1:
Empty the condensed milk into a container and stir in the vanilla extract. Whip the cream until it holds in soft peaks before gently folding into the condensed milk.

Step 2:
Spoon the mixture into a freezer proof container and freeze until just solidifying. Remove from the freezer, pour over the maple syrup and fudge pieces and fold into the ice cream mixture.

Step 3:
Freeze for another 3 hours or overnight until the ice cream becomes solid.
