Doughnut Holes

About the bake

Our doughnut hole recipe is a great way to use up the centers of your ring doughnuts.

Within the recipe, the dought holes are deep-fried in a saucepan filled with vegetable oil and then glazed with sugar.

Why not serve them with a caramel sauce for a really indulgent treat.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

500g    Self-raising white flour

1tsp    Baking powder

90g     Billington's Unrefined Golden Caster Sugar

2tbsp  Milk

2pcs   Medium Eggs

2tbsp  Sunflower oil

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Deep fryer

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Sift together the flour and baking powder into a bowl and stir in the sugar. Beat together the eggs, oil and milk then add to the dry ingredients. Mix together until a smooth dough forms.

Step 2:
Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and roll out to about 1cm thickness. Cut circles out of the dough using a small, floured cutter.

Step 3:
In a deep saucepan heat some vegetable oil to about 170°C then fry the doughnuts 2-3 at a time by carefully placing in the oil for 3-4 minutes until they are golden brown on the bottom. Then turn them over and cook for a further 2-3 minutes until golden in colour and cooked through. Drain on kitchen paper.

Step 4:
Whilst still warm roll the doughnuts in some extra caster sugar.

Step 5:
Eat on the same day or keep in an airtight container and eat within 1-2 days.

Total time: 35mins
Prep Time: 25mins
Bake time: 10mins
Serving: 6

Doughnut Sticks

About the bake

Our doughnut sticks recipe with dipping chocolate on the side is quick and easy, ideal for a Sunday afternoon treat or for a birthday party. They are best eaten freshly made whilst still hot.

If you enjoyed making our donut sticks with dipping chocolate, we think you'll love our nutella filled doughnut holes.

*This recipe requires a deep fat fryer

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

250g    Self-raising white flou

0.5tsp; Baking powder

40g     Billington's Unrefined Golden Caster Sugar

2tbsp  Milk

1pc   Medium Eggs

2tbsp  Milk

150ml  Vegetable oil


For the Dip

150g Dark chocolate chips

150ml Double cream

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Deep fryer

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Sift together the flour and baking powder into a bowl and stir in the sugar. Beat together the eggs, oil and milk then add to the dry ingredients. Mix together until a smooth dough forms.

Step 2:
Beat one tablespoon of oil and egg into into the dry ingredients, adding the milk slowly until the mixture forms a soft dough.

Step 3:
Heat the remaining oil in a deep-fat fryer to 170°C (335°F) or until a little of the mixture sizzles as soon as it is dropped in.

Step 4:
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and pat out to about 1 cm thick. Cut into thin strips and place carefully into the hot oil. Fry for 3-4 mins on each side, until golden and crisp.

Step 5:
Sprinkle the doughnuts with the sugar and cinnamon while hot and serve as soon as possible.

Step 6:
To make the chocolate dip, gently warm the chocolate and cream together until melted. Pour into a serving dish and serve

Total time: 23mins
Prep Time: 15mins
Bake time: 8mins
Serving: 24

Easy Banna Bread

About the bake

If you're looking for an easy banana bread recipe then look no further. Banana loaf is such a great way of using over ripe bananas, the fruit adds moisture and sweetness to your bake. This recipe can be frozen once baked. Just slice beforehand so you can defrost a slice at a time. If love the warming undertones of spice in your loaf, try replacing a tbsp of flour for ginger. It's a gamechanger! Love a loaf? Try out lemon loaf cake recipe next.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

135g    Unsalted butter (softened)

175gp    Caster Sugar

1tsp     Nielsen-Massey Vanilla Extract

1tbsp  Milk

2pcs   Large Eggs

2tbsp  Sunflower oil

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Preheat the oven to 170'c (150'c fan).

Step 2:
Grease and line a 2lb loaf tin

Step 3:
In a large bowl or a stand mixer, cream together the butter and golden caster sugar on a high speed until light and fluffy (around 3 minutes)

Step 4:
Add the eggs one at a time, whisking together between each addition.

Step 5:
Add the bananas and vanilla extract to the bowl and combine.

Step 6:
In a separate bowl, add the self raising flour and ground cinnamon and gently stir together.

Step 7:
Add the dry ingredients to the wet a little at a time and whisk very gently on a low speed until fully combined and no lumps remain.

Step 8:
If the mixture is a little thick, add a tablespoon of milk to loosen.

Step 9:
Pour the mixture into the prepared loaf tin and top with the third banana, sliced in half lengthways to decorate.

Step 10:
Bake in the preheated oven for around 1 hour or until an inserted skewer comes out clean.

Step 11:
Once fully baked, remove from the oven and leave to cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then turn out to cool fully on a wire rack.

Step 12:
Once cooled, slice and serve.

Total time: 1h
Prep Time: 25mins
Bake time: 35mins
Serving: 6

Ginger & Orange Loaf

About the bake

Loaves don't just have to be savoury. Banana bread is famously known as a lockdown classic, so we present you to, Ginger and Orange bread. With sweet hints of orange and a subtle hot kick of ginger, this loaf is equally morish and moist. Easy to make in 6 steps - slice after slice you'll keep coming back to this flavoursome bread.

The dough can also be divided into smaller bread tins to make Mini Ginger and Orange loaves - perfect for parties or smaller portions.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

175g    Unsalted butter (softened)

175g  Unrefined Light Muscovado Sugar

75g     Golden syrup

75g  Black treacle

1pc   Large Egg

3 tsp  Ground ginger

150ml  Vegetable oil

300ml  Whole milk

175g  Self Raising Flour

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Deep fryer

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Put the flours, salt, ground & glace ginger, orange zest and yeast into a bowl.

Step 2:
Mix to a soft dough with the orange juice, butter, egg, milk, treacle and water

Step 3:
Turn onto a floured surface and knead for 10 minutes.

Step 4:
Shape into a loaf shape and put into a 2lb loaf tin, which has been greased.

Step 5:
Leave to prove until it has doubled in size. Preheat the oven to 220°C (fan 200°C, gas mark 7)

Step 6:
Place in the oven and then immediately turn the heat down to 200°C (fan 180°C, gas mark 6). Bake for 30-35 minutes.
