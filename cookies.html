Chocolate Chip Millie's Cookies

About the bake

This recipe is as close as we can get to our favourite Millie's cookies. For the perfect soft, chewy texture, bake them for no more than 7 minutes. Take them out and let them set as they harden up a bit more. If you prefer them a little harder, just leave them in the oven for a couple more minutes.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

12g Unsalted butter(softened)

115g Billington's Unrefined Light Muscovado Sugar

110g Silver Spoon White Caster Sugar

1 Medium egg

1tsp Nielsen-Massey Vanilla Extract

220g Allinson's Self Raising Flour

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Deep fryer

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Preheat the oven to 200°C, (fan 180°C, gas mark 4) and line 2 baking trays with baking parchment.

Step 2:
In a large bowl cream together the butter and sugars until pale and fluffy then add the egg and vanilla extract and mix together well.

Step 3:
Sift the flour and salt and then add to the egg mixture. Mixing thoroughly until a soft dough forms. Finally stir in the chocolate chips.

Step 4:
Break off walnut sized pieces of the dough, and roll into balls. Place these on the baking trays leaving space between them as they will spread out during baking.

Total time: 35mins

Serving: 24

Honey & Raisin Oat Cookies

About the bake

These oat cookies with raisins and honey are quick, easy and delicious. Enjoy as cookies or why not serve with ice cream for a special treat!

In just 35 minutes you'll have made a batch of 12 delicious fudge.

Ingredients

250g    Self-raising white flour

0.5tsp; Baking powder

40g     Billington's Unrefined Golden Caster Sugar

2tbsp  Milk

1pc   Medium Eggs

2tbsp  Milk

150ml  Vegetable oil


For the Dip

150g Dark chocolate chips

150ml Double cream

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Deep fryer

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Sift together the flour and baking powder into a bowl and stir in the sugar. Beat together the eggs, oil and milk then add to the dry ingredients. Mix together until a smooth dough forms.

Step 2:
Beat one tablespoon of oil and egg into into the dry ingredients, adding the milk slowly until the mixture forms a soft dough.

Step 3:
Heat the remaining oil in a deep-fat fryer to 170°C (335°F) or until a little of the mixture sizzles as soon as it is dropped in.

Step 4:
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and pat out to about 1 cm thick. Cut into thin strips and place carefully into the hot oil. Fry for 3-4 mins on each side, until golden and crisp.

Step 5:
Sprinkle the doughnuts with the sugar and cinnamon while hot and serve as soon as possible.

Chocolate Peanut Butter Cups

About the bake

If you have a sweet tooth then you are sure to love these adorable chocolate peanut butter cups by Kate Doran creator of Thelittleloaf.com. Smooth peanut butter encased in a blend of dark and milk chocolate, these would make a great gift for a loved one or an indulgent treat just for you! This recipe features in Homemade Memories: Childhood Treats with a Twist by Kate Doran. Available in stores now.

Click Here: Method & Ingredients

Ingredients

500g    Self-raising white flour

1tsp    Baking powder

90g     Billington's Unrefined Golden Caster Sugar

2tbsp  Milk

2pcs   Medium Eggs

2tbsp  Sunflower oil

Utensils

Measuring cups and spoons.

Stand mixer.

Large mixing bowl.

Doughnut cutter

Oven

Wire rack

Deep fryer

Tongs, wooden chopsticks, or spider.

Method

Step 1:
Place the flour, sugar and cocoa powder in a microwaveable mug and stir together until well mixed.

Step 2:
Stir in the oil and boiling water and stir together until the mixture is smooth.

Step 3:
For the Nutella mug cake: Spoon the Nutella into the middle of the mixture. For the Biscoff mug cake: Spoon the Biscoff Spread into the middle of the mixture. For the Lindt mug cake: Push the Lindt Lindor Truffles into the middle of the mixture.

Step 4:
Spoon the cake mixture over the filling so it’s concealed.

Step 5:
Microwave for 1 minute* until the cake is risen and has started to pull away from the edge of the mug.

Step 6:
Top the mug cake with delicious decorations, grab a spoon and dig in!
